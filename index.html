Agar — Fluid Gel ›
0.5%Soft, pourable, loose — good for sauces & fluid applications
1.0%Sweet spot — sets firm, blends smooth and glossy
1.5%Stiffer set, very stable — good for piping or structured applications
1.5–2.0%Heat stable — holds on a hot plate through service, won't melt until ~185°F
1Hydrate agar in cold liquid. Bring to a full boil — must hit 185°F+ to activate properly.
2Season and finish as needed. Pour into shallow pan. Let set completely.
3Blend in VitaPrep on high until completely smooth and fluid. Pass through fine tamis if needed.
Xanthan Gum — Sauce Body ›
0.1%Very light — barely noticeable viscosity, just a touch of body
0.2–0.3%Sweet spot — adds body, prevents syneresis, still pours cleanly
0.5%+Thick, gel-like — starts to feel gluey if pushed further
1Always shear in — whisk vigorously or use VitaPrep on high. Will not hydrate properly by hand stirring.
2Shear-thinning: flows under pressure, thickens back at rest — ideal for plating.
Gelatin — Ratios & Usage ›
0.5–1%Soft set — glazes, lightly gelled sauces, velouté body
1.5–2%Firm set — panna cotta, terrines, molded gels
1 silver sheet≈ 2.5g. Melts at ~95°F — not heat stable, do not use on hot plates
1Bloom sheets in cold water 5–10 min. Wring out excess. Melt into warm liquid (not boiling).
2Set in refrigerator minimum 2 hours, overnight preferred. Not heat stable above ~95°F.
Methocel F50 — Hot Gels ›
1.5–2%Standard hot gel — sets when heated, melts when cold (reverse-sets)
KeyMust hydrate in cold liquid below 50°F before use
1Disperse into cold liquid using high shear — will not hydrate in warm liquid.
2Chill in ice bath ~10–15 min until smooth. Sets firm above ~130°F, returns to liquid when cooled.
Ultra-Tex 8 — Cold Thickening ›
1–3%Adjust to desired consistency — thickens without heat
KeyModified tapioca starch — shear in with VitaPrep for smooth result
1Add to cold or room temp preparation. Shear in using VitaPrep — do not hand whisk.
2Thickens instantly. Very stable — holds well under refrigeration, does not weep.
